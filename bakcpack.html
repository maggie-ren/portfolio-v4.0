Backpack, A mobile application that sends school and class reminders, with adjustable times for receiving them. Parents/guardians would also have consent forms, a take-home folder, school events, and more at their fingertips.

Timeline and contribution

Two weeks project. Team of five. I did user research, definition and ideation, led the creation of the wireframes and prototype, interview and usability testing.

Role

UX Designer, UX Researcher

Background

Parent shaming has become so prevalent in recent years that it’s been making headlines. Who hasn’t had their child-rearing choices questioned by family and friends maybe even an ex-spouse and their new partner? Unsolicited ‘advice’ or ‘suggestions’ have a way of creeping into your psyche promoting shame where it once was not. Several articles state that specifically working parents struggle to keep up with all of their work life balance, child-rearing demands, specifically their children’s education, and is compounded with each additional child.

User Insight

Parents with young children have high anxiety about missing out on announcements, permission slips, missing assignments, and other important school events. Parents need a balanced and simplified way to be alerted about future assignments, important school events and student safety because having a structured agenda allows them to properly plan more family time and feel more secure when their children is out of reach.

Problem Statement

We found that Parents aren't being alerted enough about their children's personal school life, ex: nursing visits, school schedule, field trip notices, which can cause anxiety for many parents. How might we create a platform that allows an easy way for parents to be notified about personal information regarding their children that will ease their anxiety? Backpack is designed for parents to be notified by schools and teachers about latest events, closures, and important updates regarding their children.

Hypothesis Statement

We believe if a parent is able to readily access up-to-date school information, a child’s opportunity for educational success dramatically improves. Providing parents with a clear, organized, way to view and participate in their child’s education (real-time) allowing for direct feedback with teachers will reduce parental anxiety and connect parent(s) to child(ren) while improving overall home stability. Our mobile application will influence families to move toward a proactive task-management model, and improve the family education dynamic in the home.

User-focused Motivation

This product is valuable because, often times it's difficult to access and find general and important information about what happens in the school. Often times parents/guardians get second hand and sometimes no information from their children. Having a tool that showcases major elements of what is happening in the child's educational life would be useful.

Proto-persona

We designed a proto-personal to identify the average users behavior and pain points.

User Research

We multiple in-person interviews on a target group. Besides initial research questions we also asked more detailed questions. For example: What worries you the most when your child is in school? What aspects of your child's school life do you want to be more aware of? Do you often communicate with teachers about future assignments? if yes, how? Do you avoid communication with the school teacher/admin? Why? What form of communication would be most comfortable? Can you tell me a time you forgotten/ missed a school activity and left your child behind (maybe, change last part to “ activity… and your child was left out”)

Interview Points

We pulled the keywords from the interview and made each keyword a focus point for research and potential improvement.

“I check my kid’s student portal everyday, if not multiple times per day. I don’t like to miss things.”

Affinity Diagram

Eight different focus points were created from the interview results. These points will be heavy referenced when creating our future user-persona.

User Persona

From this research, we’ve consolidated a user persona, Leia Kingston, a divorced mother of two wonderful kids. Her work life keeps her busy, but her kids keep her even busier. Between work and kids, Leia can feel disorganized and overwhelmed with keeping track of her kids and fears that she is insufficient as a parent. We believe with our application, Backpack, we can alleviate her anxieties and allow for Leia to worry less about keeping her children’s academic matters orderly and in place. Instead she can spend more time with her kids. User persona also can help us to define a user insight and the problem statement.

Feature Prioritization Matrix

After we conducted a Competitor Analysis, Our team brainstormed using the mind map "I like, I wish, What if" and synthesize the ideas down to three key features using a feature prioritization matrix. They are: I wish I would know what my child worked on that day so I could talk to my kids about specific things that happened during their day, knowing when school event days are and to be able to pick and choose which notification is on.

Task Flow

We seperated the user flow into 9 task flows to analyze the user’s behavior and this helped us understand what features of this app are the user's primary needs.

Draft Wireframes

With much of our research, we got to sketching out our ideas. We centered our focus on three key features presented on a dashboard. A challenge we faced was expanding on certain features like “Today” being slightly broad. We solved this issue by adding in a “Daily activities” and “School Events” button on the dashboard so that users have more insight on what to navigate. This also made the app more interactive.

Digital Wireframes

Anticipated Outcome are: Organized experience with child’s education. Always informed on child’s progress and mental health. No more paper and the worry of it getting lost. The child has a better experience overall with their education.
