Cooking Matters is helping to end childhood hunger by inspiring and educating families to make healthy and affordable food choices. After researching different non-profit organizations, our group found out we had one major fact in common: we LOVE food! Also, with two people in our team having backgrounds working with children, we found a great combination of both food and educating children in the organization, Cooking Matters.

Timeline and contribution

Two weeks project. Team of five. I did user research, definition and ideation, UI design including creating high-fidelity wireframes and prototype, interview and usability testing.

Role

UX/UI Designer

User Insight

Our users need to find resources for efficient and economical recipes with relevant nutritional information to meet their needs.

Problem Statement

Constrained by both time and budget, our users need resources for efficient and economical recipes that will support them in providing abundant nutrition for themselves and their families.

Hypothesis Statement

We believe that by providing efficient and economical recipes with tips for relevant nutritional information and education, Cooking Matters will support users in cultivating healthy food habits.

Cooking Matters Site Now

As you can see here these are some of the updates we feel will help their company, which we assessed while compiling our heuristic evaluation. We want to update their site’s appearance to help the user’s overall experience with the process, while also helping establish the Cooking Matters brand. By simplifying their navigation, reorganizing the information and making the site also mobile friendly, we hope to help cooking matters continue to build their brand and recognition.

Research

Because our non-profit is located in Washington, DC and did not have a direct phone number for contact on their site - it was a little difficult to get in touch and speak with someone on the phone. We were however able to reach them via email and were excited to receive a response with not only their acknowledgement of the much needed updates for their site, but also a copy of their current proposal for a site redesign. The proposal was extremely helpful in that it provided us with much of the information we would have obtained during an interview. We found their main participants are parents, caregivers, and healthcare workers along with WIC. They also want to provide food education and accessible resources for all their participants, and motivate them to make healthy choices.

Affinity Diagram

We then conducted interviews to find out a little more about people/families habits when it came to cooking, grocery shopping, nutrition and budget… In summary, we found that people like to have an idea of what they will be cooking (from a recipe or at least starting from there), timely meals, and making grocery shopping more cost-effective

“Time is limited, too busy to cook and buy groceries but must allot time for grocery and cooking.”

“Eating in is healthier and cheaper than eating out. My son has celiac disease, and there are only certain places that the family can go eat together.”

Current Website Map

To solve these problems we started by mapping out the Cooking Matters website by documenting the various pages, the path ways to navigate these pages, the visuals and content in order to discover the website’s shortcomings including: color contrasts particularly the type links to pages that do not function properly if at all and confusion caused by how menu categories and links were organized so we updated the range of colors used for the website’s identity and reorganized the menu and footer links using a card sorting method.

Card Sorting

The updated menu arranges all the pages across four categories and is used for both the Main menu and Footer options. We also planned out areas for social media, a link for the home page, and call to actions such as donating to Cook Matters.

Storyboard

In this User Storyboard, Kelly is a working mother of two children, one of whom has a severe dairy allergy. Limited by both time and budget, she has found it challenging to shop for groceries and to cook for her family. Referred to the Cooking Matters website by a friend, Kelly finds a repository of recipes and helpful nutrition education resources that support her in making nutritious and delicious meals for her family that meet their needs.

Userflow

Due to the time limitation, we decided to only develop recipes and educational tools sections.

Iterstions

Four rounds of feedback and improvement was done with users for both desktop and mobile wireframes to create a better experience.

UI Style Guide

The original website color combinations did not pass the color contrast accessibility tests, so we decided to find some colors we liked and at the same time make sure they pass the tests. For the outcome, we kept the original website's main color green, found some colors to remind us of delicious foods like yellow and orange as well as used natural color for the background.

Digital Wireframes

To further improve our website, we want to add ‘send as text’ to mobile function, add additional filters (i.e most recent and most viewed), add an interactive map as well as add a chatbot to direct people.
